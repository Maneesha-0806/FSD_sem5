COFFEE

Coffee is a beverage brewed from roasted, ground coffee beans. Darkly colored, bitter, and slightly acidic, coffee has a stimulating effect on humans, primarily due to its caffeine content, but decaffeinated coffee is also commercially available. There are also various coffee substitutes.

Types of Coffee

  1. Espresso: A strong, concentrated coffee showcasing the pure flavor of the beans.
  2. Cappuccino: Equal parts espresso, steamed milk, and milk foam.
  3. Café Latte: 1/3 espresso and 2/3 steamed milk, often topped with a thin layer of foam.
  4. Americano: A shot of espresso diluted with hot water.
  5. Flat White: Similar to a latte, but made with micro-foam (steamed milk with tiny bubbles) for a smoother, denser texture.
  6. Macchiato: A shot of espresso "stained" or marked with a dollop of foamed milk.
  7. Mocha: Espresso mixed with steamed milk and chocolate syrup or powder.
  8. Cortado: An even 1:1 ratio of espresso and steamed milk, designed to cut the bitterness.
  9. Brewed & Filter Coffees
    • Drip/Filter Coffee: Ground beans steeped in hot water and filtered, commonly brewed in machines.
    • French Press: A coarse grind steeped in hot water, then separated using a metal mesh plunger.
    • South Indian Filter Coffee: A staple in your region, made by brewing finely ground dark roast coffee powder with chicory in a traditional metal filter, then mixed with frothy milk and sugar.
  10. Iced & Chilled Coffees
    • Iced Coffee: Freshly brewed hot coffee poured over ice.
    • Cold Brew: Coffee grounds steeped in cold water for 12–24 hours, resulting in a smooth, highly caffeinated, and less acidic flavor.
    • Frappé: A blended iced coffee drink, often made with instant coffee, milk, and sweet syrups.
